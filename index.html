Professional Biography

My journey into the field of information science has been shaped by both school and work, and the two have blended together in ways that define how I approach problems. I am finishing my Bachelor of Science in Information Science at the University of South Florida, with a concentration in web design technologies, and I expect to graduate in December 2025. Throughout this program I have built skills in web development, information architecture, data security, and user experience.

At the same time I have been working as a full stack software engineer, which has allowed me to apply classroom learning on real projects. Courses such as Introduction to Information Science (LIS 3261), IT Concepts for Information Professionals (LIS 3353), and Information Policy and Ethics (LIS 4414) helped me understand how information moves through organizations and highlighted the ethical responsibilities that come with it. Design focused classes, including WWW Page Design and Management (LIS 3361), Interaction Design (LIS 3352), and Information Architecture (LIS 3783), pushed me to think about usability and accessibility in every project.

Professionally, I work at ActionAtlas as a full stack developer using React, Node.js, and MongoDB to deliver cross functional features for a project management platform. Earlier freelance and contract work exposed me to Django, Salesforce, Shopify, and other tools. These experiences reinforced Agile practices, version control, secure deployment, and collaboration across roles.

Looking ahead, I am interested in roles such as full stack engineer, solutions architect, or project lead, with ongoing learning in cloud infrastructure and security. Above all, I want to use my skills to create technology that is ethical, accessible, and genuinely useful to the people who rely on it.
